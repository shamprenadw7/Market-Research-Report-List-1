<p><p>The passenger car friction material market primarily consists of brake linings, brake pads, and clutch facings. Brake linings are attached to the drum brakes, providing stopping power by generating friction. Brake pads serve a similar function for disc brakes, clamping against the rotor for effective deceleration. Clutch facings are employed in manual transmissions, facilitating smooth engagement and disengagement of the engine power. Each component is critical for vehicle safety and performance, with materials engineered for durability and heat resistance.</p></p>
