<p><ul><li>Miosis Drugs</li><li>Beta-Blocker</li><li>Adrenergic Agonists</li><li>Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitor</li><li>Prostaglandin Derivative Agent</li><li>Penetrates</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The anti-glaucoma drug market comprises several types of medications targeting intraocular pressure reduction. Miosis drugs enhance pupil constriction to improve drainage. Beta-blockers decrease aqueous humor production, effectively lowering pressure. Adrenergic agonists stimulate receptors to enhance fluid outflow and reduce production. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors inhibit enzyme action, further decreasing fluid production. Prostaglandin derivative agents increase uveoscleral outflow, making them highly effective. Together, these categories offer diverse treatment options to manage glaucoma and preserve vision.</p></p>
