<p><p>The camera wireless flash market consists of two primary types: internal and external flashes. Internal flashes are built into the camera body, offering convenience for casual photography, but with limited power and versatility. In contrast, external flashes are standalone units that can be mounted on the camera or used remotely, providing higher output, adjustable settings, and better control over lighting conditions. The choice between these types depends on the photographer's needs, ranging from spontaneous shooting to professional studio setups.</p></p>
